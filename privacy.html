ReplyKit — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-22

ReplyKit is designed to be private by default. We (the developer) do not run any server that receives your snippets, and we do not sell, rent, or share your data.

What we collect

ReplyKit itself does not collect or transmit your snippets to us. We have no analytics, no trackers, and no advertising. Two optional Pro features involve data leaving your device — both described below.

Where your snippets live

  • Free plan: the snippets you create are stored locally in your browser using the standard chrome.storage.local API. They stay on your device.
  • Pro plan (sync): to keep your snippets available across your devices, they are saved using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.sync API. This syncs them through your browser's own sync service (e.g. your Google account, if you are signed in to Chrome with sync enabled), under your browser vendor's privacy policy. They are never sent to us or to any third-party server we control.

Payments (Pro)

Pro subscriptions are processed by ExtensionPay (which uses Stripe for payment processing). When you subscribe, ExtensionPay/Stripe handle your email address and payment details to manage your subscription. ReplyKit never sees or stores your card data. See the ExtensionPay and Stripe privacy policies for details on how they process this information.

Permissions

  • Storage — to save your snippets locally (chrome.storage.local) and, for Pro users, to sync them across your devices (chrome.storage.sync).
  • Host access / activeTab — required so the extension can insert a selected snippet into the text field of the page you are actively using. ReplyKit only reads or writes text in a field when you explicitly trigger it (typing //, pressing the shortcut, or clicking a snippet). It does not read, log, or transmit page content.

Analytics & tracking

ReplyKit contains no analytics, no trackers, and no advertising.

Changes

If a future version processes data in a new way, this policy will be updated to reflect it.
