A free text expander alternative for Chrome

Updated June 2026

If you keep retyping the same replies, a text expander saves real time — but most are paid or heavy. ReplyKit is a free, lightweight alternative: save a snippet once, then insert it anywhere by typing //.

Text expanders let you store reusable text (greetings, FAQ answers, follow-ups, sign-offs) and drop it into any field with a short trigger. The classic tools — TextExpander, aText and similar — are powerful but often come with a subscription, a desktop install, or a learning curve. If all you want is quick canned replies in your browser, that can be overkill.

ReplyKit focuses on doing one thing well: fast text snippets, right where you type, with nothing to learn.

How it works

  • Save your most-used replies as snippets (a title and the text).
  • In any text field, type // (or press a keyboard shortcut).
  • Pick a snippet from the instant search — it's inserted at your cursor.
  • Or hit Copy to paste it manually in editors that block auto-insert.

It works in Gmail, help desks, marketplaces, social networks, CRMs and ordinary web forms — anywhere with a standard text field.

When a lightweight tool is enough

Full-featured expanders shine if you need fill-in forms with branching logic, shared team libraries, or system-wide expansion across native desktop apps. Most people don't. If your repetitive typing happens in the browser — email, support tools, marketplaces, social, forms — a lightweight extension covers the job without the subscription or the setup. You can always graduate to a heavier tool later; starting simple means you actually build the habit instead of abandoning a complicated one.

Snippets worth saving first

The fastest way to feel the time savings is to capture the handful of things you already retype every day:

  • Your greeting and sign-off, as separate snippets you can mix and match.
  • Answers to your three most common questions.
  • Your calendar or booking link.
  • A polite "I'll look into this and get back to you" holding reply.
  • Boilerplate you paste often — an address, payment details, a standard disclaimer.

Five snippets is enough to notice the difference in the first afternoon.

How much time does a text expander save?

It adds up faster than it feels. If you retype ten short replies a day at roughly fifteen seconds each, that's two and a half minutes daily — over ten hours a year on phrases you could insert in a keystroke. For anyone in support or sales handling dozens of messages, the savings multiply. And it isn't only about speed: not rewriting the same message keeps your tone consistent and frees your attention for the parts that actually need thought.

Switching from another text expander

Moving over is painless: there's nothing to import or configure. Install ReplyKit, recreate your five or ten most-used snippets by hand (a few minutes), and assign a shortcut to the ones you reach for constantly. Snippets are stored locally on the free plan, so there's no account to set up and nothing leaves your browser. If you later upgrade to Pro, they sync across your devices through your browser's built-in sync.

Frequently asked questions

What is a text expander?
A text expander stores reusable text — greetings, FAQ answers, sign-offs — and inserts it into any field with a short trigger, so you stop retyping the same things.

Is there a free text expander for Chrome?
Yes. ReplyKit is free for up to 10 snippets, with no account needed. Pro unlocks unlimited snippets and sync.

How is ReplyKit different from heavier text expanders?
It's lightweight and browser-first: no desktop install, no account on the free plan, and it focuses on quick // insertion in any web text field.

Does it work outside the browser?
ReplyKit is a Chrome extension, so it works in any standard web text field. For desktop apps you'd need a system-wide expander instead.
